Living Labs outdoor education programme

"Connecting young South Africans to their natural heritage, one river at a time"

Philosophy

Living Labs is a positive reaction to the problem of growing global disconnect between young people and nature, as well as the challenge of conserving river ecosystems – arguably South Africa’s most valuable natural resource. It is a reaction based on the premise that meaningful relationships between communities and the local environment are the strongest foundation for committed conservation of the valuable, but vulnerable ecosystems and biodiversity with which we share this planet.

Vision

Living Lab’s vision is to work with our partners to grow and sustain a strategic network of schools, each committed to supporting the education and development of young South Africans in the context of river health, sustainability, and biodiversity.

Soil moisture sensor

A soil moisture sensor measures water content in the soil using technologies like capacitive or resistive methods. It provides real-time data to aid in irrigation management, agriculture, and landscaping, helping optimize water usage and improve plant health.

Tracking tool

An animal tracking tool is a device or system used to monitor the location and movement of animals. These tools often use GPS to collect data, which can aid in animal theft and understanding animal behavior.

Automate drip irrigation system

An automated drip irrigation system is a method of watering plants that delivers precise amounts of water directly to the root zone through a network of tubing and emitters. This technology uses timers, sensors, and sometimes weather data to control the watering schedule, ensuring efficient water use and minimizing waste.
